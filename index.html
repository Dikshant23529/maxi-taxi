FAQ

Questions we hear often

Can't find your answer? Call us on 0400 000 000 anytime — we're always here.

How do I qualify for the 75% government subsidy?
+
The WA Government Subsidised Taxi Scheme (GSTS) is available to residents with a permanent disability that prevents them from using public transport. Apply through Transport for WA with a supporting letter from your doctor. Once approved, you receive a taxi subsidy card — just show it to your Perth Max In Wheelchair driver and we handle the rest.
How many wheelchairs can one vehicle carry?
+
Our wheelchair-accessible vehicles comfortably carry up to 2 wheelchairs secured with certified tie-down systems, plus 4–5 additional seated passengers. For larger groups or multiple wheelchair users, we can arrange a convoy booking — just let us know when you book.
What is your on-time airport guarantee?
+
We guarantee to arrive at your pickup address on schedule for airport runs. We monitor your flight status in real time. If we are late due to our error and you miss your flight, we will contribute to reasonable costs incurred. Full details available on request.
Can my support worker travel with me at no extra cost?
+
Yes. One support worker or carer can travel free of charge in any wheelchair taxi booking. Additional support workers are charged at the standard per-passenger rate. Simply mention your support worker when booking so we prepare the vehicle correctly.
Do you service all Perth suburbs and regional WA?
+
We cover all metro Perth suburbs and many surrounding regional areas of Western Australia. Use our Service Areas section to check your suburb, or call us — if we can't service your area directly, we'll connect you with a trusted partner provider.
How quickly can I get a wheelchair taxi?
+
For on-demand bookings, we aim to have a driver with you within 20–30 minutes in most metro areas. For peak times, airport runs, or complex needs, we recommend pre-booking at least 2–3 hours in advance. We also offer recurring weekly bookings for regular travel.
